# Humaniser Training Guide: Building a Field-Specific Corpus

To get the most out of Humaniser, you should train it on actual research papers from your specific academic field. This allows the engine to learn the specific "linguistic DNA" of your discipline.

## 1. Where to Download Academic Papers
You need high-quality, human-written PDFs. Avoid papers that were written or heavily edited with AI.

- **[arXiv.org](https://arxiv.org/):** Best for Computer Science, Physics, Mathematics, and Quantitative Biology.
- **[PubMed Central (PMC)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/):** Best for Medicine, Life Sciences, and Biology.
- **[ERIC](https://eric.ed.gov/):** Best for Education research and Social Sciences.
- **[DOAJ](https://doaj.org/):** Directory of Open Access Journals across all fields.

## 2. How to Run `build_corpus.py`
The `build_corpus.py` script automates the ingestion of a large folder of PDFs.

1.  **Organize your PDFs:** Put all papers for a specific field into a folder (e.g., `~/Downloads/CS_Papers`).
2.  **Start the servers:** Ensure `./start.sh` is running.
3.  **Run the script:**
    ```bash
    cd humaniser/backend
    python3 build_corpus.py /path/to/your/pdf/folder --field computer_science
    ```

## 3. Recommended Corpus Size
- **Minimum:** 20 papers (roughly 400-600 pages).
- **Ideal:** 50+ papers.
- **Target:** Aim for at least 2,000 unique sentences in your style profile.

## 4. Understanding the Quality Report
After the script finishes, it will print a **Quality Report**.

- **Average Score < 30:** **Excellent.** The papers you provided are highly human-like. The Humaniser will learn very natural patterns.
- **Average Score 30 - 40:** **Good.** Standard academic writing.
- **Average Score > 45:** **Warning.** The papers you fed in are being flagged as AI-generated by our detector. This will "pollute" your style profile and make the humanization results worse. **Review your source papers.**

## 5. Managing Profiles
If you want to start over for a specific field:
1.  Go to `http://localhost:8001` (Trainer UI).
2.  Find the field (e.g., "computer science") and click **Delete**.
3.  Re-run the `build_corpus.py` script with your improved selection of papers.
